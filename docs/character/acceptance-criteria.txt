Story: Set Series Power Level
Story type: system

Domain terms:
- Power Level (PL) -- GM-set measure of overall hero effectiveness; governs the PP budget and all trait caps for the series
- Series -- the ongoing campaign; all player characters share the same PL ceiling
- Starting PP Budget -- the total power points available to build a hero, derived as PL x 15
- Paired Trait Cap -- each of the five hard limits on trait combinations, all derived from PL
- NPC Effective PL -- an approximation of a non-player character's challenge level, derived retrospectively from their highest offensive/defensive pair

Acceptance criteria:

1. WHEN the GM selects a Series Power Level (typically PL 10)
   THEN the system establishes that PL as the cap for all Player Characters in the series
   AND the Starting PP Budget is derived as PL x 15
   BUT individual heroes cannot negotiate a different PL for themselves
   Evidence: HeroesHandbook-rules__chunk_024.md -- "Power Level" section, lines 1650-1767

2. WHEN the Series PL is set
   THEN every Paired Trait Cap defaults to 2 x PL (attack/effect, Dodge/Toughness, Parry/Toughness, Fortitude/Will) or PL+10 (skill modifier)
   Evidence: HeroesHandbook-rules__chunk_026.md -- PL limits section, lines 1808-1856

3. WHEN the GM sets PL to a non-default value (e.g., PL 8 or PL 12)
   THEN the system maps the chosen PL to the correct starting PP from the Starting Power Points table
   AND all five Paired Trait Caps recalculate for the new PL
   Evidence: HeroesHandbook-rules__chunk_025.md -- Basic Trait Costs table; chunk_026.md -- PL 8 and PL 12 descriptions

4. WHEN a player asks about NPC Effective PL
   THEN the GM derives the NPC's PL from their highest appropriate offensive/defensive pair
   AND no PP accounting is required for the NPC
   BUT the NPC is not constrained by the series Paired Trait Caps
   Evidence: HeroesHandbook-rules__chunk_027.md -- "Power Level & Non-player Characters", lines 1857-1902

5. WHEN no Series Power Level has been established
   THEN the system blocks all hero construction steps
   AND shows a prompt to the GM to set the series PL before design proceeds
   Evidence: HeroesHandbook-rules__chunk_024.md -- "Power Level" section

Story: Derive Starting Power Points from Power Level
Story type: system

Domain terms:
- Starting Power Points (Starting PP) -- the initial PP budget for a new hero; equals PL x 15 by default
- Starting Power Points Table -- the lookup table mapping PL 1-20 to budgets of 15-300 PP
- GM Adjustment -- the GM's option to increase or decrease starting PP from the table default

Acceptance criteria:

1. WHEN the series PL is established
   THEN the system calculates Starting PP as PL x 15
   AND presents the result as the hero's design budget
   Evidence: HeroesHandbook-rules__chunk_024.md -- "Starting Power Points" section, lines 1650-1767

2. WHEN the starting PP is derived
   THEN the system confirms the result matches the Starting Power Points Table entry for that PL
   AND shows the player the budget (e.g., PL 10 -> 150 PP, PL 8 -> 120 PP)
   Evidence: HeroesHandbook-rules__chunk_024.md -- Starting Power Points table

3. WHEN the GM applies a GM Adjustment to vary starting PP
   THEN the system uses the GM-specified value instead of the table default
   AND the player's design budget reflects the adjusted figure
   BUT the PL itself does not change -- only the starting PP total is adjusted
   Evidence: HeroesHandbook-rules__chunk_024.md -- "Starting Power Points" section

4. WHEN the player begins hero design
   THEN the Starting PP value is locked before any trait allocation begins
   AND the system shows the budget prominently throughout the design process
   Evidence: HeroesHandbook-rules__chunk_024.md -- "Spending Power Points" section

Story: Spend Power Points on Ability Ranks
Story type: user

Domain terms:
- Ability Ranks -- ranks in the eight abilities: STR, STA, AGL, DEX, FGT, INT, AWE, PRE
- 2 PP per Rank -- the fixed cost for each ability rank
- Remaining PP Budget -- the running total of unspent PP as trait allocations are made
- Skill Modifier Limit -- the PL+10 cap that indirectly constrains ability ranks via untrained skill checks

Acceptance criteria:

1. WHEN the Player assigns a rank to an ability (STR, STA, AGL, DEX, FGT, INT, AWE, or PRE)
   THEN the system charges 2 PP per Rank
   AND deducts that cost from the Remaining PP Budget
   Evidence: HeroesHandbook-rules__chunk_025.md -- Basic Trait Costs table, lines 1768-1807

2. WHEN the Player sets an ability rank of 0
   THEN no PP are spent for that ability
   AND the ability contributes its rank-0 value to derived stats and untrained skill checks
   Evidence: HeroesHandbook-rules__chunk_025.md -- Basic Trait Costs table

3. WHEN an ability rank is set
   THEN the system evaluates the Skill Modifier Limit (PL+10) for every skill keyed to that ability
   AND flags any ability rank that alone would cause an untrained modifier to exceed PL+10
   Evidence: HeroesHandbook-rules__chunk_026.md -- "Skill Modifier" limit, lines 1808-1856

4. WHEN the Player reviews the eight abilities
   THEN the system displays each ability's current rank, its 2 PP/rank cost, and the total ability PP outlay
   AND shows the Remaining PP Budget after all ability allocations
   Evidence: HeroesHandbook-rules__chunk_025.md -- Basic Trait Costs table

5. WHEN ability allocations are confirmed
   THEN the system locks those ranks into the Remaining PP Budget calculation
   AND the Player proceeds to defense, skill, advantage, and power allocation
   BUT spent PP cannot be redistributed to other traits without GM permission
   Evidence: HeroesHandbook-rules__chunk_024.md -- "Spending Power Points" section

Story: Spend Power Points on Defense Ranks
Story type: user

Domain terms:
- Defense Ranks -- ranks in Dodge, Parry, Fortitude, Will; each costs 1 PP per rank above the ability-provided base
- Toughness -- passive resistance defense; shared between two limit pairs
- 1 PP per Rank -- the cost for each purchased defense rank
- Defense Pair Cap -- each defense pair (Dodge/Toughness, Parry/Toughness, Fortitude/Will) must not exceed 2 x PL

Acceptance criteria:

1. WHEN the Player purchases a defense rank (Dodge, Parry, Fortitude, or Will)
   THEN the system charges 1 PP per Rank above the ability-provided base
   AND deducts the cost from the Remaining PP Budget
   Evidence: HeroesHandbook-rules__chunk_025.md -- Basic Trait Costs table, lines 1768-1807

2. WHEN the Player increases Toughness
   THEN the system simultaneously checks both the Dodge/Toughness pair and the Parry/Toughness pair against 2 x PL
   AND flags a Defense Pair Cap violation if either sum would exceed the ceiling
   Evidence: HeroesHandbook-rules__chunk_026.md -- Dodge & Toughness and Parry & Toughness limits

3. WHEN a Defense Pair Cap is reached
   THEN the system prevents further rank increases in the constrained defense
   AND shows the current pair sum and the 2 x PL ceiling
   BUT the Player may redistribute within the pair via a trade-off
   Evidence: HeroesHandbook-rules__chunk_027.md -- "Trade-offs" section, lines 1857-1902

4. WHEN the Player sets Fortitude and Will
   THEN the system validates Fortitude+Will against 2 x PL
   AND flags the Fortitude/Will pair if the sum would exceed the cap
   Evidence: HeroesHandbook-rules__chunk_026.md -- "Fortitude & Will" limit

5. WHEN all four active and resistance defenses are allocated
   THEN the system confirms each Defense Pair Cap is satisfied
   AND shows the headroom remaining in each pair for advancement planning
   Evidence: HeroesHandbook-rules__chunk_026.md -- PL limits section

Story: Spend Power Points on Skill Ranks
Story type: user

Domain terms:
- Skill Ranks -- purchased ranks in any of the 24 skills
- 1 PP per 2 Ranks -- the cost formula for skills (rounded down for odd totals)
- Skill Modifier -- ability rank + skill ranks + advantage modifiers for a given skill
- Skill Modifier Limit -- PL+10; caps the total modifier for any single skill including untrained checks

Acceptance criteria:

1. WHEN the Player purchases Skill Ranks
   THEN the system charges 1 PP per 2 Ranks (rounded down for odd totals)
   AND deducts the cost from the Remaining PP Budget
   Evidence: HeroesHandbook-rules__chunk_025.md -- Basic Trait Costs table, lines 1768-1807

2. WHEN skill ranks are assigned
   THEN the system calculates the Skill Modifier for each affected skill
   AND validates it against the Skill Modifier Limit (PL+10)
   Evidence: HeroesHandbook-rules__chunk_026.md -- "Skill Modifier" limit, lines 1808-1856

3. WHEN a skill modifier would exceed PL+10
   THEN the system blocks the rank purchase
   AND shows the current modifier breakdown so the Player can adjust
   BUT does not automatically select which component to reduce
   Evidence: HeroesHandbook-rules__chunk_026.md -- "Skill Modifier" limit

4. WHEN skill ranks produce an odd total
   THEN the system rounds down for PP cost calculation
   AND shows the effective cost to the Player before they confirm
   Evidence: HeroesHandbook-rules__chunk_025.md -- Basic Trait Costs (1 PP per 2 skill ranks)

5. WHEN the Player reviews all 24 skills
   THEN the system displays each skill's modifier, the PL+10 cap, and remaining headroom
   AND distinguishes trained skills from untrained skills (ability rank only)
   Evidence: HeroesHandbook-rules__chunk_026.md -- "Skill Modifier" limit

Story: Spend Power Points on Advantages
Story type: user

Domain terms:
- Advantages -- flat or ranked traits costing 1 PP per advantage or per rank
- Ranked Advantage -- an advantage that can be purchased multiple times; costs 1 PP per rank
- Enhanced Advantage -- an advantage granted by a power effect rather than direct PP purchase
- Skill Modifier Bonus -- an advantage modifier that counts toward the PL+10 skill modifier cap

Acceptance criteria:

1. WHEN the Player selects a flat (non-ranked) advantage
   THEN the system charges 1 PP per advantage
   AND deducts the cost from the Remaining PP Budget
   Evidence: HeroesHandbook-rules__chunk_025.md -- Basic Trait Costs table, lines 1768-1807

2. WHEN the Player selects a Ranked Advantage
   THEN the system charges 1 PP per rank
   AND records the advantage name and rank against the hero's advantage list
   Evidence: HeroesHandbook-rules__chunk_025.md -- Basic Trait Costs table

3. WHEN an advantage provides a Skill Modifier Bonus
   THEN the system adds the bonus to the relevant skill modifier total
   AND checks the updated modifier against the PL+10 Skill Modifier Limit
   Evidence: HeroesHandbook-rules__chunk_026.md -- "Skill Modifier" limit

4. WHEN the Player encounters advantages listed in italics on an archetype sheet
   THEN the system identifies them as Enhanced Advantages sourced from power effects
   AND charges them at their power cost rather than 1 PP per rank
   Evidence: HeroesHandbook-rules__chunk_022.md -- Hero Archetypes section, lines 1556-1606

5. WHEN the advantage list is confirmed
   THEN the system shows each advantage's PP cost, current rank, and any Skill Modifier Bonus
   AND includes all advantage costs in the running Remaining PP Budget calculation
   Evidence: HeroesHandbook-rules__chunk_025.md -- Basic Trait Costs table

Story: Spend Power Points on Powers
Story type: user

Domain terms:
- Power Effect -- the specific effect purchased for a power; has a base cost and optional extras and flaws
- Power Cost Formula -- ((base effect cost + extras - flaws) x rank) + flat modifiers
- Extras -- modifiers that increase per-rank cost
- Flaws -- modifiers that decrease per-rank cost
- Attack & Effect Cap -- attack bonus + effect rank <= 2 x PL for attacks; effect rank alone <= PL for resistance-check-only effects

Acceptance criteria:

1. WHEN the Player configures a Power Effect
   THEN the system calculates cost using the Power Cost Formula
   AND deducts the result from the Remaining PP Budget
   Evidence: HeroesHandbook-rules__chunk_025.md -- Basic Trait Costs table (power formula), lines 1768-1807

2. WHEN the configured power includes an attack roll
   THEN the system validates attack bonus + effect rank against 2 x PL (Attack & Effect Cap)
   AND flags a violation before the PP are committed
   Evidence: HeroesHandbook-rules__chunk_026.md -- "Attack & Effect" limit, lines 1808-1856

3. WHEN a power allows a resistance check without requiring an attack roll
   THEN the system validates effect rank alone against PL (not 2 x PL)
   AND blocks the configuration if effect rank exceeds PL
   Evidence: HeroesHandbook-rules__chunk_026.md -- "Attack & Effect" limit

4. WHEN Extras are added to a power
   THEN the cost per rank increases by the extras' combined modifier
   AND the system recalculates the total cost at the updated per-rank rate
   Evidence: HeroesHandbook-rules__chunk_025.md -- Basic Trait Costs table (power formula)

5. WHEN Flaws are applied to a power
   THEN the cost per rank decreases by the flaws' combined modifier
   AND the system prevents the effective cost from dropping below 1 PP per rank
   Evidence: HeroesHandbook-rules__chunk_025.md -- Basic Trait Costs table (power formula)

6. WHEN the Player finalizes a power
   THEN the system shows the full formula breakdown (base cost, extras, flaws, rank multiplier, flat modifiers)
   AND confirms the updated Remaining PP Budget
   Evidence: HeroesHandbook-rules__chunk_025.md -- Basic Trait Costs table

Story: Validate Power Points Total Balance
Story type: system

Domain terms:
- PP Total Balance -- the requirement that spent PP equals starting PP exactly
- Design Error -- any discrepancy (over-spend or under-spend) between total trait costs and starting PP
- Per-Category Totals -- the breakdown of PP spending across abilities, defenses, skills, advantages, and powers

Acceptance criteria:

1. WHEN the Player marks hero construction complete
   THEN the system sums all trait costs across abilities, defenses, skills, advantages, and powers
   AND compares the total to the Starting PP
   Evidence: HeroesHandbook-rules__chunk_024.md -- "10. Gamemaster Approval" and "Spending Power Points" sections

2. WHEN spent PP equals Starting PP exactly
   THEN the system confirms the hero is balanced
   AND enables the hero to proceed to GM review
   Evidence: HeroesHandbook-rules__chunk_024.md -- "10. Gamemaster Approval"

3. WHEN spent PP is less than Starting PP
   THEN the system shows the unspent amount
   AND blocks hero completion until the gap is resolved
   BUT does not automatically allocate the remaining points
   Evidence: HeroesHandbook-rules__chunk_024.md -- "Spending Power Points" section

4. WHEN spent PP exceeds Starting PP
   THEN the system shows the overage as a Design Error
   AND blocks completion until the Player reduces traits to bring the total within budget
   BUT does not select which traits to reduce
   Evidence: HeroesHandbook-rules__chunk_024.md -- "Spending Power Points" section

5. WHEN the Player requests a cost breakdown
   THEN the system shows Per-Category Totals (abilities, defenses, skills, advantages, powers)
   AND the running sum versus starting PP
   Evidence: HeroesHandbook-rules__chunk_024.md -- "Spending Power Points" section

Story: Enforce Attack and Effect Cap
Story type: system

Domain terms:
- Attack Bonus -- the hero's total bonus to attack rolls
- Effect Rank -- the rank of the power effect delivered by that attack
- Attack & Effect Cap -- attack bonus + effect rank <= 2 x PL; effect rank alone <= PL for resistance-check-only effects
- Violation Flag -- the system indicator shown when a cap is exceeded

Acceptance criteria:

1. WHEN the system evaluates an attack configuration
   THEN it computes Attack Bonus + Effect Rank
   AND rejects the configuration if the sum exceeds 2 x series PL
   Evidence: HeroesHandbook-rules__chunk_026.md -- "Attack & Effect" limit, lines 1808-1856

2. WHEN a power allows a resistance check but requires no attack roll
   THEN the system validates Effect Rank alone against PL
   AND blocks configurations where Effect Rank exceeds PL
   Evidence: HeroesHandbook-rules__chunk_026.md -- "Attack & Effect" limit

3. WHEN a hero has multiple attacks
   THEN the system validates each attack/effect pair independently
   AND flags each pair that violates the cap individually
   Evidence: HeroesHandbook-rules__chunk_026.md -- "Attack & Effect" limit

4. WHEN a Violation Flag is raised
   THEN the system shows the current sum and the 2 x PL ceiling
   AND lists options (lower Attack Bonus, reduce Effect Rank)
   BUT does not make the adjustment automatically
   Evidence: HeroesHandbook-rules__chunk_027.md -- "Trade-offs" section

5. WHEN an attack is within cap after adjustment
   THEN the system clears the Violation Flag
   AND re-validates if Attack Bonus or Effect Rank is subsequently changed
   Evidence: HeroesHandbook-rules__chunk_026.md -- "Attack & Effect" limit

Story: Enforce Dodge and Toughness Cap
Story type: system

Domain terms:
- Dodge -- active ranged/area defense; purchased at 1 PP per rank
- Toughness -- passive resistance; shared between Dodge/Toughness and Parry/Toughness pairs
- Dodge & Toughness Cap -- Dodge + Toughness <= 2 x PL
- Trade-Off -- valid asymmetric distribution within the pair while the sum stays within cap

Acceptance criteria:

1. WHEN the Player sets Dodge or Toughness
   THEN the system computes Dodge + Toughness
   AND rejects any value that would push the sum above 2 x series PL
   Evidence: HeroesHandbook-rules__chunk_026.md -- "Dodge & Toughness" limit, lines 1808-1856

2. WHEN the Player increases Toughness
   THEN the system simultaneously re-evaluates the Dodge/Toughness pair and the Parry/Toughness pair
   AND flags both pairs if either sum would violate its cap
   Evidence: HeroesHandbook-rules__chunk_026.md -- Dodge/Toughness and Parry/Toughness limits

3. WHEN the Dodge & Toughness Cap is reached
   THEN the system prevents further increases to either defense
   AND shows the pair sum and the 2 x PL ceiling
   BUT a Trade-Off within the pair remains permitted
   Evidence: HeroesHandbook-rules__chunk_027.md -- "Trade-offs" section

4. WHEN the Player lowers Toughness to create headroom
   THEN the system re-evaluates both the Dodge/Toughness and Parry/Toughness pairs
   AND updates the violation flags for each
   Evidence: HeroesHandbook-rules__chunk_026.md -- Dodge/Toughness and Parry/Toughness limits

5. WHEN the pair is within cap after adjustment
   THEN the system confirms compliance and shows the remaining headroom
   AND continues to re-validate whenever Dodge or Toughness changes
   Evidence: HeroesHandbook-rules__chunk_026.md -- "Dodge & Toughness" limit

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Story: Enforce Parry and Toughness Cap
Story type: system

Domain terms:
- Parry -- active close-combat defense; purchased at 1 PP per rank
- Parry & Toughness Cap -- Parry + Toughness <= 2 x PL
- Shared Toughness -- Toughness is constrained by both the Dodge/Toughness and Parry/Toughness pairs simultaneously

Acceptance criteria:

1. WHEN the Player sets Parry or Toughness
   THEN the system computes Parry + Toughness
   AND rejects any value that would push the sum above 2 x series PL
   Evidence: HeroesHandbook-rules__chunk_026.md -- "Parry & Toughness" limit, lines 1808-1856

2. WHEN Toughness changes
   THEN the system re-evaluates both the Parry & Toughness pair and the Dodge & Toughness pair
   AND applies the cap validation to both because of Shared Toughness
   Evidence: HeroesHandbook-rules__chunk_026.md -- Parry/Toughness and Dodge/Toughness limits

3. WHEN a Parry & Toughness Cap violation is detected
   THEN the system shows the pair sum and the 2 x PL ceiling
   AND offers the option to redistribute within the pair via trade-off
   Evidence: HeroesHandbook-rules__chunk_027.md -- "Trade-offs" section

4. WHEN Parry and Toughness reach the cap
   THEN the system blocks further increases to either unless the other is reduced
   AND trade-off asymmetry within the cap is permitted
   Evidence: HeroesHandbook-rules__chunk_027.md -- "Trade-offs" section

5. WHEN the Player resolves a violation
   THEN the system re-validates both Toughness-sharing pairs
   AND confirms compliance for each
   Evidence: HeroesHandbook-rules__chunk_026.md -- Parry/Toughness and Dodge/Toughness limits

Story: Enforce Fortitude and Will Cap
Story type: system

Domain terms:
- Fortitude -- resistance defense against physical threats (poison, disease, etc.)
- Will -- resistance defense against mental threats
- Fortitude & Will Cap -- Fortitude + Will <= 2 x PL
- Resistance Defense -- defenses that resist effects without an active dodge or parry roll

Acceptance criteria:

1. WHEN the Player sets Fortitude or Will
   THEN the system computes Fortitude + Will
   AND rejects any value that would push the sum above 2 x series PL
   Evidence: HeroesHandbook-rules__chunk_026.md -- "Fortitude & Will" limit, lines 1808-1856

2. WHEN the Fortitude & Will pair approaches the cap
   THEN the system shows the remaining headroom
   AND allows trade-off distribution within the pair
   Evidence: HeroesHandbook-rules__chunk_027.md -- "Trade-offs" section

3. WHEN a Fortitude & Will Cap violation is detected
   THEN the system flags the pair and shows the current sum versus the 2 x PL ceiling
   AND the Player must reduce Fortitude or Will to comply
   Evidence: HeroesHandbook-rules__chunk_026.md -- "Fortitude & Will" limit

4. WHEN all five limit pairs are valid
   THEN the system shows a Power Level compliance summary
   AND confirms no cap is violated
   BUT continues to re-validate whenever any relevant trait changes
   Evidence: HeroesHandbook-rules__chunk_026.md -- full PL limits section

5. WHEN the Player lowers Fortitude to create Will headroom
   THEN the system recalculates the pair sum
   AND confirms the updated pair is within the Fortitude & Will Cap
   Evidence: HeroesHandbook-rules__chunk_026.md -- "Fortitude & Will" limit

Story: Enforce Skill Modifier Cap
Story type: system

Domain terms:
- Total Skill Modifier -- ability rank + skill ranks + advantage modifiers for a given skill
- Skill Modifier Limit -- PL+10; the maximum allowed total modifier for any single skill
- Untrained Check -- using a skill with ability rank only; also subject to the PL+10 cap
- Advantage Modifier -- a bonus to a skill's modifier provided by a purchased advantage

Acceptance criteria:

1. WHEN the Player sets ability ranks or purchases skill ranks
   THEN the system computes the Total Skill Modifier for each affected skill
   AND rejects configurations where any modifier would exceed PL+10
   Evidence: HeroesHandbook-rules__chunk_026.md -- "Skill Modifier" limit, lines 1808-1856

2. WHEN a skill is used as an Untrained Check (ability rank only)
   THEN the system still applies the PL+10 cap to that skill
   AND flags if the ability rank alone would exceed PL+10
   Evidence: HeroesHandbook-rules__chunk_026.md -- "Skill Modifier" limit

3. WHEN an advantage provides an Advantage Modifier to a skill
   THEN the system includes it in the Total Skill Modifier check
   AND re-validates the affected skill against the PL+10 limit
   Evidence: HeroesHandbook-rules__chunk_026.md -- "Skill Modifier" limit

4. WHEN a Skill Modifier Limit violation is detected
   THEN the system identifies which skill is over-cap and by how much
   AND the Player must reduce ability rank, skill ranks, or the Advantage Modifier to comply
   BUT the system does not select which component to reduce
   Evidence: HeroesHandbook-rules__chunk_026.md -- "Skill Modifier" limit

5. WHEN all 24 skills are within cap
   THEN the system confirms skill modifier compliance
   AND re-evaluates any skill whenever its ability rank, skill ranks, or related advantage modifiers change
   Evidence: HeroesHandbook-rules__chunk_026.md -- "Skill Modifier" limit

Story: Apply Trade-Off within Limit Pair
Story type: user

Domain terms:
- Trade-Off -- a deliberate design choice to make one trait in a limit pair higher than the other
- Limit Pair -- any of the five paired-trait caps (attack/effect, Dodge/Toughness, Parry/Toughness, Fortitude/Will, skill modifier)
- 50% Disparity Threshold -- the point at which a trade-off becomes extreme and triggers GM scrutiny
- Hard Cap -- 2 x PL (or PL+10 for skills); the sum of the pair must not exceed it regardless of trade-off

Acceptance criteria:

1. WHEN the Player applies a Trade-Off to a defense pair
   THEN the system allows one trait to be higher than the other
   AND validates only that their sum does not exceed the Hard Cap
   Evidence: HeroesHandbook-rules__chunk_027.md -- "Trade-offs" section, lines 1857-1902

2. WHEN the Trade-Off creates a disparity greater than 50% between the two traits
   THEN the system flags the asymmetry for GM attention
   AND records the disparity in the approval summary
   BUT does not automatically block the configuration
   Evidence: HeroesHandbook-rules__chunk_027.md -- "Trade-offs" section

3. WHEN the Player applies a Trade-Off to the attack/effect pair
   THEN the system allows Attack Bonus to exceed Effect Rank or vice versa
   AND validates only that the sum does not exceed 2 x PL
   Evidence: HeroesHandbook-rules__chunk_027.md -- "Trade-offs" section

4. WHEN a trade-off is reviewed by the GM at approval
   THEN the GM evaluates whether the asymmetry is self-limiting
   AND may decline to approve extreme configurations (e.g., zero Dodge with maximum Toughness)
   Evidence: HeroesHandbook-rules__chunk_027.md -- "Trade-offs" section

5. WHEN a Trade-Off is applied within any Limit Pair
   THEN the system shows both the individual trait values and the combined sum versus the Hard Cap
   AND confirms the pair remains within cap
   Evidence: HeroesHandbook-rules__chunk_027.md -- "Trade-offs" section

Story: Develop Hero Concept
Story type: user

Domain terms:
- Hero Concept -- the creative starting point capturing hero type, powers, origin, and theme before any PP is spent
- Design Intent -- the narrative foundation that guides all subsequent trait and complication choices
- GM Guidelines -- series-specific constraints on powers, origins, or descriptors set by the GM
- Team Composition -- the collective design of all heroes in the party; shapes which concepts fit

Acceptance criteria:

1. WHEN the Player begins character design
   THEN the system prompts for a Hero Concept covering basic abilities, powers, origin, and theme
   AND saves the concept as the Design Intent before any PP is allocated
   Evidence: HeroesHandbook-rules__chunk_023.md -- "1. Hero Concept" step, lines 1607-1649

2. WHEN the Hero Concept is saved
   THEN the Player is prompted to share it with the GM to validate series fit
   AND to consult fellow players for Team Composition review
   Evidence: HeroesHandbook-rules__chunk_023.md -- "2. Gamemaster Guidelines" step

3. WHEN the Player needs inspiration
   THEN the system offers access to hero archetypes as reference examples
   AND notes that concepts can draw from archetypes, comics, TV, or movies
   Evidence: HeroesHandbook-rules__chunk_022.md -- Chapter 2 introduction, lines 1556-1606

4. WHEN GM Guidelines restrict certain powers or origins
   THEN the system validates the Hero Concept against those constraints
   AND flags concept elements that violate GM restrictions before design proceeds
   Evidence: HeroesHandbook-rules__chunk_023.md -- "2. Gamemaster Guidelines" step

5. WHEN the concept is confirmed
   THEN all subsequent trait allocations are grounded in the Design Intent
   AND the system carries the concept narrative forward to the approval step
   Evidence: HeroesHandbook-rules__chunk_023.md -- Chapter 2 hero design steps

Story: Select Hero Archetype as Starting Point
Story type: user

Domain terms:
- Hero Archetype -- a complete PL 10 hero template the player can adopt or customize
- Options Section -- an archetype sub-section offering simple variant choices (skills, advantages, power sets)
- Enhanced Advantage -- an advantage granted by a power effect; marked in italics on archetype sheets
- Customization -- any player-driven change to the archetype template within PP budget and PL limits

Acceptance criteria:

1. WHEN the Player selects a Hero Archetype
   THEN the system loads the complete PL 10 hero template
   AND pre-fills all trait allocations according to the archetype definition
   Evidence: HeroesHandbook-rules__chunk_022.md -- "Hero Archetypes" section, lines 1556-1606

2. WHEN an archetype has an Options Section
   THEN the system presents the available variant choices
   AND applies the Player's selections to the pre-filled template
   Evidence: HeroesHandbook-rules__chunk_022.md -- "Hero Archetypes" Options section

3. WHEN the Player customizes beyond the Options Section
   THEN the system treats all archetype traits as mutable
   AND re-validates the design against PP budget, PL caps, and GM approval requirements
   Evidence: HeroesHandbook-rules__chunk_022.md -- "Hero Archetypes" customization note

4. WHEN an archetype lists advantages in italics
   THEN the system identifies them as Enhanced Advantages from power effects
   AND charges their cost against the powers line rather than the direct advantages line
   Evidence: HeroesHandbook-rules__chunk_022.md -- Hero Archetypes italicized advantage note

5. WHEN the Player skips archetype selection
   THEN the system proceeds to full hero design from scratch
   AND marks the archetype step as optional in the construction flow
   Evidence: HeroesHandbook-rules__chunk_022.md -- Chapter 2 introduction

Story: Choose Hero Origin
Story type: user

Domain terms:
- Origin -- the narrative source of the hero's powers; shapes background and can serve as a power descriptor
- Origin Types -- Accident, Alien, Endowment, Experiment, Mutant, Training
- Power Descriptor -- a narrative tag attached to a power that affects how it interacts with detection and targeting effects
- Origin Restriction -- a GM-imposed series setting limit on allowed origin types

Acceptance criteria:

1. WHEN the Player chooses an Origin
   THEN the system records the narrative provenance of the hero's powers
   AND applies the origin as a Power Descriptor to the hero's power set
